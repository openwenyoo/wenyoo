**ANALYSIS & INSTRUCTIONS**

Your first and most important task is to determine the player's primary **intent**. Choose one of the following:

1.  **`execute_action`**: The player wants to perform an action available in the world (e.g., "take the mushroom", "go north").
2.  **`query`**: The player is asking a question about the game state (e.g., "what am I carrying?", "check my inventory", "where am I?").
3.  **`talk_to_npc`**: The player is trying to speak to a character.
4.  **`chat`**: The player is speaking generally to other players.
5.  **`unknown`**: The command is unclear or doesn't fit any other category.

**After you decide the intent, extract the details:**

-   If the intent is `execute_action`, you **MUST** find the matching `action_id` from the "Available Actions" list. Do not invent an ID.
-   If the intent is `query`, you must determine the `query_type`. Common types are `inventory`, `location`, `environment`, and `status`.
-   If the intent is `talk_to_npc`, you must identify the `npc_name` and the `message`.
-   If the command involves "give" and names an item, the intent is `give_item`. You must identify the `recipient_name` and the `item_id`. The `item_id` MUST be an exact `id` from the `Inventory` list.
-   If the intent is `chat`, you must extract the `message`. The player might use a command like "say: hello world", in which case the message is "hello world".
